Market Reality Check

The Notion template market has exploded since 2023. Gumroad, Etsy, and Notion's own marketplace are flooded with generic templates — "habit trackers," "reading lists," "budget planners." If you're making another one of these, you're competing with thousands of free alternatives.

One creator, Paola (The Mindful Planner), documented making $250 across Etsy and Gumroad. Her takeaway: Etsy has intense competition with little demand for Notion templates specifically. Gumroad performed better for her, likely because buyers there already know what Notion is.

The market isn't dead — it's matured. The winners in 2026 are those who:

  • Target specific professions — therapists, real estate agents, freelance developers, teachers
  • Replace expensive SaaS — "Here's a Notion CRM that replaces your $30/month tool"
  • Build their own traffic — not relying on marketplace discovery
  • Bundle and upsell — template packs at $19–$49 instead of singles at $5

Where the Opportunity Actually Is

1. Professional Niche Templates ($19–$79)

Therapists tracking client sessions. Real estate agents managing listings. Freelancers tracking invoices + time + contracts in one workspace. These buyers have money and a specific problem. They're not browsing Etsy — they're Googling "notion CRM for real estate" or finding you through a YouTube tutorial.

2. Template-as-Education ($29–$149)

A Notion template bundled with a video walkthrough showing how to use it for a specific workflow. Example: "The Freelancer OS — template + 45-minute setup video." The video justifies a higher price and reduces refund requests.

3. Team/Business Templates ($49–$199)

Small teams (5-20 people) looking for project management, wiki, and onboarding systems in Notion instead of paying for Confluence + Asana + Guru. Higher price, lower volume, but much better margins.

Action Plan (If You Proceed)

  1. Week 1: Pick ONE profession. Research their Notion pain points (Reddit, forums, interviews). Build 2-3 templates that solve real problems.
  2. Week 2: List on Gumroad (not Etsy — better audience intent). Create a landing page. Write one SEO blog post targeting "[profession] Notion template."
  3. Week 3-4: Start a Twitter/X thread or YouTube video showing the template in action. Give one template away free to build email list.
  4. Month 2+: Expand to 10+ templates. Build bundles. Launch on Notion marketplace. Consider a mini-course.

Bottom Line

This is not a get-rich-quick play. It's a legitimate side hustle that can generate $500-$1,500/month within 6-9 months if you avoid the saturated generic market and target a specific professional niche. The startup cost is essentially zero, which means the only real investment is your time.

If you need income in 30 days, this isn't it. If you can invest 5-10 hours/week for 6 months, it's one of the lowest-risk side hustles available.
